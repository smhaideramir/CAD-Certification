# Reflection on CAD Basic Certificate

This CAD certification was my first structured introduction to technical CAD work and engineering drawings.

## What I learned

- How a mechanical component is represented in a CAD workflow
- How 3D parts are translated into 2D technical drawings
- How dimensions, section views, and detail views are used to communicate geometry
- Why clear documentation matters in engineering and experimental work

## Challenges

The most challenging part was learning to think about the part not only as a 3D object, but as something that must be manufactured, measured, and communicated clearly through a technical drawing.

## Relevance to physics

As a physics student, I see CAD as useful for experimental and laboratory work. Many research setups require simple mechanical parts, holders, mounts, enclosures, or alignment structures. Even a basic understanding of CAD makes it easier to communicate with technical staff, understand instrument design, and contribute to experimental setups.
